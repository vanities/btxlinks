Mining centralization risk. A share this large raises 51%-attack and censorship risk for the whole chain — consider pointing hardware at a smaller pool.

    Shares are each pool's own live estimate — its measured hashrate divided by its own difficulty-based guess of the network rate, then normalized to 100%. They're approximate, not exact, and a fast-growing network makes the estimates run high. For actual block-win counts (who really found the blocks), see the Byron explorer's 24h mining view ↗.

    What is BTX?

    BTX is a post-quantum hard fork of Bitcoin Knots. It replaces traditional hashing proof-of-work with matrix multiplication — work that maps cleanly onto GPUs and CPUs — and swaps ECDSA signatures for quantum-resistant, lattice-based cryptography.
